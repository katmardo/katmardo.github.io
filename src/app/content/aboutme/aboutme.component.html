In 2017, I co-founded an aerospace education program, Destination SPACE (Satellite Program for Aerospace-Centered Education) with my supervisor and two colleagues from a NASA internship. Our goal is to offer hands-on, stimulating curriculum for students from backgrounds underrepresented in the aerospace field. We began by offering a week-long summer program where students built their own weather sensors, launched them on weather balloons, and analyzed the data.

Five years later, Destination SPACE has expanded to offer instruction in four states and has partnered with several agencies and institutions in order to continuing reaching diverse and talented students. I have gained experience developing and teaching curriculum, writing grants, planning events, communicating with collaborators, and mentoring students.

One of my first jobs out of college was working with Wilderness Inquiry, an organization committed to making outdoor education and recreation accessible to everyone, regardless of income, experience, or physical or cognitive abilities. As an Outdoor Leader, I helped participants gain confidence in outdoor skills, such as canoeing, fire building, camping, cooking, and leadership.

I have also been a teaching assistant for four college semesters. As an undergrad I TA'ed two photography courses, in which I helped students learn Photoshop, organize their portfolios, and develop film. In grad school, I was lucky enough to teach a lab section for Weather and Climate twice. I enjoyed encouraging students to become interested in climate science and meteorology, as I tried my best to keep lab sessions fun and informative.
